Research Interests & Expertise

I am a dedicated PhD candidate in Industrial Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology, working under the supervision of Dr. Benoit Montreuil. My research focuses on the intersection of Physical Internet, Supply Chain Engineering, and Logistics, where I develop robust protocols, agent-based simulation models, and innovative tools for multi-echelon supply chain cooperation.

Currently, I am working on Net Zero Freight Systems, focusing on sustainable practices and energy efficiency in freight transportation. My work includes designing and implementing strategies for achieving net-zero emissions through alternative fuels, vehicle electrification, and route optimization. I collaborate extensively with industry partners to develop innovative freight solutions that align with global sustainability goals.

My experience spans various high-impact projects including regional distribution center optimization with Steelcase Inc., critical PPE supply systems during COVID-19, network redesign for multinational logistics with SF-Express, and multimodal transportation optimization with UPS. Through these projects, I've developed expertise in advanced optimization models, Linux-based deployment systems, and autonomous supply chain technologies.
